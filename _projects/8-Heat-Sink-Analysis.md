This project is for MAE 3240, Heat Transfer.  It involves analyzing heat transfer, efficiency, and effectiveness across a cylindrical pin fin, and then applying this fin to a 5x5 fin array, making a heat sink.  The efficiency and thermal resistance of the heat sink is found and used to analyze the effect of using a heat sink to disperse heat from a thin film heater.

This project utilizes lots of important heat tranfer concepts, including thermal conduction, convection, tip conditioning, fin performance and arrays, and thermal resistance networks.  This project allows me to combine lots of simpler heat transfer calculations and workflows into a more complex problem that is more applicable to real world applications.  For example, while this problem utilized only vague fin arrays and a thin film heater, this very setup could resemble a computer chip, where the chip itself is the heater and the heat sink interfaces with the chip packaging.  The clear difference between the temperature of the heater surface with and without the fin array attached (an astonishing 89 degrees C) shows how important it is to understand heat transfer's application to product design.  This is something that I feel more confident baking into my future designs and analyses as a result of solving problems like this one.
